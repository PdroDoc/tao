115th Verse

2

3

4The ancient masters were profound and subtle.

5Their wisdom was unfathomable.

6There is no way to describe it.

7

8One can only describe them vaguely by their appearance.

9Watchful, like men crossing a winter stream.

10Alert, like men aware of danger.

11Simple, as uncarved wood.

12Hollow like caves.

13Yielding, like ice about to melt.

14Amorphous, like muddy water.

15

16But the muddiest water clears

17as it is stilled.

18And out of that stillness

19life arises.

20

21He who keeps the Tao does not want to be full.

22But precisely because he is never full,

23he can remain like a hidden sprout

24and does not rush to early ripening.
